What is SQA?

Software Quality Assurance (SQA) is a process that ensures software is developed with high quality by following defined standards, processes, and procedures.

Main Goal of SQA:

Prevent defects in software development
Ensure product meets requirements
Improve software quality and reliability
Follow standard development process

SQA Activities:

Reviewing requirements and design documents
Creating test plans and test cases
Performing different types of testing
Tracking and reporting defects
Ensuring process compliance

Types of Testing in SQA:

Manual Testing
Automation Testing
Functional Testing
Non-Functional Testing

Importance of SQA:

Reduces software errors
Saves time and cost
Improves customer satisfaction
Ensures stable and reliable product

QA Perspective:

SQA ensures quality is built into every phase of SDLC, not just testing at the end.
